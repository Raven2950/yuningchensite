Force Vector of Grief

p5.js / Interaction Design / Generative Text

NYU ITP / 2024

"I have always loved solitude... Not the solitude of forests or empty roads, but the solitude that exists in the midst of others. There is a kind of hatred that does not cry out... It grew within me, imperceptibly."

Concept

This project explores the physical manifestation of "grief" and "alienation". Text is no longer just a medium for narrative; it is transformed into dynamic particles trapped within simulated gravitational and fluid fields. The piece illustrates how emotional trauma creates invisible walls, quietly hardening and distancing us from the outside world.

Mechanism

Utilizing p5.js vector fields, I programmed the trajectory of a singular "teardrop". As this force source travels across the canvas, it disrupts the rigid structure of the text array, causing displacement, shearing, and repulsion. The letters struggle to return to their original coordinates using spring physics, symbolizing the lingering impact of a deeply rooted emotional force.
